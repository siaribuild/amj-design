Create a detailed, production-quality website prototype for a Melbourne aluminium windows and doors supply business.

Working brand name: AMJ Trade Direct.

Business model:
AMJ Trade Direct is a Melbourne-based sales channel for aluminium windows and doors manufactured by an established manufacturer. The website is not a normal e-commerce shop. It is a quote-to-order trade supply platform. Users can browse products, build an indicative quote, upload plans or a window schedule, then submit for manual review. A deposit is only paid after the quote has been manually verified and approved. The manufacturer handles production and door-to-door delivery. The business does not provide installation services.

Target customers:
1. Trades doing renovations.
2. Small builders.
3. Owner-builders and homeowners researching products, but homeowners should be guided to work with their builder or installer rather than self-installing.

Design direction:
Use the attached (print.pdf) SIARI BUILD visual language as inspiration - no need to follow exactly, just to understand stakeholder's taste:
- Premium residential construction feel.
- Strong black/off-white/bronze palette.
- Use #111111 for black, #F5F3EF for warm off-white, #B8946A for bronze/gold accent, #C8C5BE for muted grey.
- Use bold uppercase architectural headings.
- Use a technical, confident, trade-friendly tone.
- Use angular clipped buttons and subtle angular line details.
- Use high-quality realistic imagery of modern Melbourne homes, aluminium sliding doors, aluminium windows, double-glazed frames, building sites, renovation details and delivery/trade contexts.
- Avoid generic corporate imagery.

- Avoid copying the manufacturer’s existing website layout.
